### 1. **Race Condition in Observer List Access**
- **Vulnerability Type**: Race Condition / Thread Safety Issue
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Location**: Multiple methods accessing `observers_` without thread safety guarantees
- **Exploitable**: Yes, in multi-threaded scenarios

**Details**:
- The `observers_` member is accessed from multiple threads:
  - `AddObserver()` and `RemoveObserver()` are called on the origin thread
  - `GetConfigCompleted()` also accesses `observers_` but is called via `PostTask` from a worker thread
- While `GetConfigCompleted()` is posted to the origin thread, there's no guarantee about when it runs relative to `AddObserver`/`RemoveObserver` calls
- The `ObserverList` is marked as `Unchecked`, which means no thread safety checks

**Risk**: An observer could be added/removed while `GetConfigCompleted` is iterating over the list, potentially causing:
- Use-after-free if an observer is deleted during iteration
- Iterator invalidation
- Memory corruption

### 2. **Potential Use-After-Free in `GetConfigCompleted`**
- **Vulnerability Type**: Use-After-Free
- **Severity**: Medium
- **Location**: `Core::GetConfigCompleted()` method
- **Exploitable**: Yes, under specific timing conditions

**Details**:
